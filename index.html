Overarching Goals for this event

Empower educational learning through Mozilla's web-makers tool and other open source tools ( Develop a Webmakers teaching Kit to guide communities through Webmakers events - https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/top-tips-creating-great-teaching-kits).

Explore how Open Data can be used to develop the countries in the region, review various platforms and explore how open data can be improved.

Explore how to put strong , diverse and thriving open source communities in our region through Mozilla resources.

Recruit at-least 15 new contributors, especially for FSA.

Atleast 4 FSA host Webmaker events subsequently, SAY every after 2 months in collaborations with their hub.

Assign 2 members of the community to lead Localization projects through hubs support for Mozilla products (FirefoxOS & Firefox) etc. http://mozilla.locamotion.org/projects/firefoxos/

Hosting Monthly MozCoffee's for community members and FSA to get community reviews on developments.

Why Bantu Tech Tour?

Bantu Tech Tour is a week long event focusing on bringing Entrepreneurs, Innovators, Developers from Namibia and Zambia in Southern African region to address issues affecting the region (Internet penetration, Open Web, Innovation & Open Source Cultures) and how the solutions brought up could leverage, revolutionize and establish active Mozilla communities and the region into an open tech hub.

Who is this tour for?

The event features various talks from Mozilla Rep, passionate Mozillians and (hands) on sessions in open tools offered by Mozilla (Webmakers Appmaker etc), Introducing Localization of Mozilla products, Firefox OS and lastly Web-makers.However this event is open to everyone who is passionate about technology, and making the Web an innovative platform and a place to find opportunities.
